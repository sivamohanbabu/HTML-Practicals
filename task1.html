Hyderabad Famous Veg Biryani

Prepare the Rice

  1. Rinse basmati rice under cold water until water runs clear.
  2. Soak rice in water for 30 minutes.
  3. Drain the soaked rice completely before cooking.

A simplified Hyderabad-style vegetarian biryani using layered rice and spiced vegetables. Serve with raita and salad.

  1. Ingredients
    1. 2 cups basmati rice, soaked 30 minutes
    2. 3 tbsp ghee or oil
    3. 1 large onion, thinly sliced
    4. 2 tomatoes, chopped
    5. 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrot, potato, peas, beans)
    6. 1/2 cup yogurt
    7. 2 tsp ginger-garlic paste
    8. 2–3 green chilies, slit
    9. Fresh mint & coriander, chopped
    10. 1/2 tsp turmeric, 1 tsp red chili powder, 1 tbsp biryani masala
    11. Whole spices: 4 cloves, 4 green cardamom, 1-inch cinnamon, 1 bay leaf
    12. Salt to taste, saffron soaked in 2 tbsp warm milk (optional)
  2. Prepare the vegetables and masala
    1. Heat ghee/oil, add whole spices until fragrant.
    2. Sauté sliced onion until golden; remove half for garnish.
    3. Add ginger-garlic paste, green chilies, tomatoes and cook till soft.
    4. Stir in vegetables, yogurt, turmeric, chili powder, biryani masala and salt. Cook until vegetables are almost done. Mix in mint and coriander.
  3. Parboil the rice
    1. Boil water with a little salt and a splash of oil. Add soaked rice and cook until 70% done (grains still firm).
    2. Drain rice and keep warm.
  4. Layering and dum (steaming)
    1. In a heavy-bottomed pot, spread half the vegetable mixture. Top with half the parboiled rice.
    2. Sprinkle some fried onions, chopped mint/coriander and a little saffron milk.
    3. Repeat with remaining vegetables and rice, finishing with fried onions and saffron.
    4. Seal the pot with a tight lid (or dough) and cook on very low heat for 20–25 minutes (dum).
  5. Finish and serve
    1. Let rest 5–10 minutes, gently fluff the biryani to mix layers.
    2. Serve hot with raita, salad or mirchi ka salan.
